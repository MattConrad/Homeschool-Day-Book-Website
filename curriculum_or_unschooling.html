Homeschool records that fit your unique style:

Not everyone homeschools the same way. And the way your family homeschools today may not be the exact way you homeschooled yesterday. Homeschool Day Book is flexible. Keep your homeschool records in the way that suits you.

If you're following a curriculum, Day Book tracks the time you spend easily and accurately. Move entries from day to day when needed. Include multiple students in the same lesson with no extra work. Use printable reports to review your lesson coverage or to gather information for reporting to others.

If you're less-structured homeschoolers, you'll like the simplicity of Homeschool Day Book. Record only what is important to you, with no unwanted requirements. Enter all your activities for the day on a single screen (see the screenshot).

Of course, the easiest way to find out if Homeschool Day Book is right for you is to try it. The trial is free, and includes all the features of the full licensed version (the only restriction is a limited number of uses).

(For unschoolers: If you're interested in the idea of unschooling software or unschooled record keeping, take a look at this article from Karen Gibson, talking about her approach to unschooling-with-record-keeping.)
